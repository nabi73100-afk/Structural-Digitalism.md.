# What is Structural Digitalism
From Lacquer as Protective Skin to Digital Subjectivity

Abstract
Structural Digitalism proposes a new ontological framework in which structure functions not as a symbolic system or interpretive model, but as a generative logic producing emergent subjects within digital environments. Drawing from the historical evolution of lacquer practices — from its initial function as a preservative coating to the autonomous structural logic of dry lacquer sculpture (夹纻) — this paper argues that contemporary digital image formation represents a continuation of material generative processes in a computational domain. By examining lacquer as both material technology and epistemic model, Structural Digitalism reconfigures the relationship between matter, structure, and subjectivity. The digital subject is thus understood not as an agent but as the phenomenal outcome of stratified generative systems. This paper distinguishes Structural Digitalism from classical structuralism, post-structuralism, and contemporary digital aesthetics by foregrounding material formation as the ontological condition of appearance.
1. Introduction
The emergence of digital image systems has fundamentally transformed the ontological status of visual phenomena. While traditional media theory has largely interpreted this transformation through frameworks of representation, simulation, or technological mediation, these approaches remain grounded in optical paradigms inherited from photography and cinema. Structural Digitalism proposes an alternative trajectory: digital visuality should be understood as a continuation of material generative logic rather than as a rupture from materiality.
This theoretical proposition draws its primary model from lacquer. Historically, lacquer was not first an aesthetic medium but a technological solution to problems of preservation and environmental resistance. Its function as a protective skin — preventing decay, corrosion, and microbial degradation — established a unique relationship between surface and structural integrity. Over time, lacquer practices evolved beyond protective coating into autonomous structural systems, particularly evident in the dry lacquer (夹纻) technique, where the lacquer membrane itself becomes the primary load-bearing entity.
This historical transition from auxiliary surface to structural subject provides a critical conceptual framework for understanding digital formation. Structural Digitalism argues that digital images similarly undergo a transformation from representational surfaces to generative structural subjects. The digital is therefore not a virtual abstraction but a domain in which material logic re-emerges through computational processes.

# 《Structural Digitalism：从漆的保护性皮肤到数字主体》全书总结性简介

本书提出“结构数字主义”（Structural Digitalism）这一全新的本体论框架，旨在重新理解从传统材料文明到当代数字文明之间的深层连续性。作者通过对东亚漆艺历史的材料学与哲学考察，指出漆最初作为一种保护性“皮肤”的功能，逐步演化为具有独立结构逻辑的人工材料体系，尤其在夹纻工艺中首次实现了“结构生成身体”的文明转折。这一历史经验，为理解当代数字图像与算法结构提供了关键的理论原型。
在此基础上，本书提出：数字图像并非现实的再现，而是通过神经网络层、渲染管线与参数空间的多重分层生成机制所形成的“表面存在”。数字世界的核心不在符号解释或虚拟模拟，而在结构性生成逻辑本身。结构不再是意义的容器，而成为主体的生成条件。由此，“数字主体”（Digital Subject）被界定为一种非生物、非心理的存在形式，它通过算法层的稳定化而获得感知上的一致性与文化上的现实效应。
全书进一步发展出“数字漆宇宙论”的观念，认为算法光、时间压缩与参数气候构成了一种新的材料宇宙。在这一宇宙中，图像、风格与感知模式具有类似历史器物与宗教图像的结构地位，数字艺术因此成为一种新的文明材料实践。最终，本书提出“算法材料主义”（Algorithmic Materialism），主张将计算过程视为一种真实的物质生成机制，从而为理解人工智能时代的艺术、文化与存在形态提供系统性的哲学基础。
